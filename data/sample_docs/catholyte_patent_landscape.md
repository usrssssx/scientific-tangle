The international patent landscape describes catholyte circulation in nickel electrowinning and diaphragm cell designs. Several electrowinning cell configurations use distributed manifolds, bottom feed and side overflow to stabilize nickel deposition.

For nickel electrowinning, optimal catholyte flow velocity is usually reported as 0.15-0.30 m/s. Flow velocity below 0.10 m/s increases concentration polarization, while flow velocity above 0.35 m/s can raise energy consumption and disturb the cathode layer.

Foreign practice from Canada and Finland reports catholyte circulation with online pH and temperature control. The finding is described in patents and pilot reports, but the exact operating window depends on nickel concentration, diaphragm resistance and impurity load.
